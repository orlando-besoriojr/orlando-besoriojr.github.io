Race and Wikidata

Summary

My project focuses on how race is represented by Wikidata. I will dive into Stuart Hall's theory of the Spectacle of the Other and query Wikidata to determine how Hall's ideas have materialized into modern day datasets.

What is the Spectacle of the Other?

The Spectacle of the Other is a theory of representation that analyzes the tendency for society to create binary oppositions. These dichotomies are tools that had "captured the diversity of the world" (Hall, 235), but at the same time reduce complexity. What happens then is that the 'other' is reduced to the differences, typically extremes, that exclude them from the rest of society. As Hall writes, it "engages feelings, attitudes and emotions and it mobilizes fears and anxiety in the viewer" (Hall, 226). These reactions materialize into misrepresentation and misunderstandings, all of which perpetuate power inequalities. This effect is especially true in the context of race, a social construct with differences that regularly change and require a dialogue to understand fully.
